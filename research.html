Research Interests

My primary research interest is developing novel theoretical and practical frameworks for making inferences on massive correlated data sets with a focus on spatial, functional, and multivariate data. The computational challenges encountered when working with massive, correlated data sets force statisticians to develop flexible models and methodologies that leverage exploitable structures in the data. To date, I have primarily focused on environmental, healthcare, precision agriculture, and electrical engineering applications. In these fields, standard statistical tools such as parametric modeling and non-parametric smoothing quickly break down due to the exponential increase in data sparsity for a fixed number of observations as data dimensionality grows. My approach to modeling and methodology is grounded in dimension reduction and variable selection methodology. This approach assumes the existence of a low-dimensional subspace that can replace the original high-dimensional space in which the data exists while still retaining all the necessary information to make inferences on the parameters of interest. This framework for modeling provides the ability to effectively screen the structure of data, which is not an easy task when the dimensionality is moderately large. In addition to dimension reduction, I have recently started developing machine learning algorithms for various applications such as modeling environmental, health, and dynamic data.

My specific research areas include:

  • Dimension Reduction
  • Functional Data Analysis
  • Machine Learning
  • Multivariate Analysis
  • Spatial and Spatiotemporal Statistics
  • Variable Selection
